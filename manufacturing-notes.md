## Box Milling
- Use the internal-clamp-fixture to have the vise clamp against the box bottom face (instead of the edges and nubs)
- Apply a lot of pressure, possibly crushing the clamp fixture box as needed, to try to get contact against the side wall
- This will chatter, likely due to the face being milled being unconstrained and long/flat and vibrating
  - Clamping a block of wood to the face, even if not otherwise attached to the fixture, seems to help, possibly just from mass to dampen vibrations
  - Go slow on the spindle, 500rpm on a 3/4" end mill seemed to chatter less

## IDEC box modifications
- Punch out the 21.3mm holes
- Dremel a 2mm deep, 1mm wide keying slot, for anti-rotation for the M12 connector
  - This does not have to go all the way through, the anti-rotation tab on the adapter is triangular 
- Print the M12 adapter with supports, ideally using dual-material breakaway-interface
